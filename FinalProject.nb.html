#Research Question African Swine fever is a viral disease that has caused major issues for the swine industry in numerous countries, including China, Vietnam, and the Phillipines. China, home to half of the world’s pigs, has been hit the hardest by African Swine fever. Countries have resorted to slaughtering their livestock and placing bans on transport of livestock in and out of the country. The media has released news about African Swine Fever very recently, but why has this news not come out earlier, when ASF was first noticed by farmers? Does the change in export numbers from mid 2018 -2019 provide insight on when ASF affects were seen? If so, how much earlier then when the news reports covered it was its effects present? We would like to explore whether or not African Swine Fever had a major impact on the swine industry before the media released reports in 2019 and around when this impact occured.

#Look at Yearly Total Meat Movement Because African Swine Fever caused so much distruption in the swine industry of many countries, we wanted to see how much the total imports and exports of each country changed over the years. After arranging the sums in descending order, we can see that Canada, Mexico, and Japan had the largest total movement of meat throughout most, if not, all of the years since 2014. When we find China further down in the dataset, we can see that total movement of meat in countries such as China, Vietnam, and the Phillipines has increased through the years because their total movement of meat increases.

Yearly_Movement_Country <- 
  LivestockYearlyClean%>%
  filter(amount_in_1000s > 0)%>%
  group_by(year,country)%>%
  summarise(total_Movement = sum(amount_in_1000s))%>%
  arrange(desc(total_Movement))

Conclusion

African Swine Fever did have an impact on the swine industry before it was released in the media. As seen in the first graphic of yearly protein movement, we saw that top countries such as Canada and Mexico experienced a decrease in protein movement or a smaller increase in protein movement than previous years.
